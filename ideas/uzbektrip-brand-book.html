01 — About This Document

Brand Overview

uzbektrip.com is a premium independent travel guide to Uzbekistan — the fastest-growing tourism destination in Central Asia. This document defines the visual and verbal identity of the brand across all touchpoints: web, print, social media, and correspondence.

Brand Essence

The Silk Road, made easy.

uzbektrip.com bridges the mythic weight of the ancient Silk Road with the practical needs of the modern traveler. Every design choice — from Lapis Blue drawn from medieval tilework to Cormorant Garamond evoking illuminated manuscripts — reflects this duality: historic grandeur, contemporary clarity.

Chapter 01

Logo & Mark

The uzbektrip wordmark pairs a calligraphic serif display font with a clean geometric emblem. The mark is an abstracted Silk Road compass — inspired by the 8-pointed Uzbek geometric star motif found in Samarkand tilework.

The Mark — Standalone Use

The 8-pointed star compass mark may be used independently as a favicon, social media avatar, app icon, or stamp. It must always appear in Lapis Blue or Silk Gold; never in other colors.

ON CREAM
ON LAPIS
ON DARK
Logo Usage Rules

Do

  • Use on approved backgrounds (cream, lapis, dark, gold-pale)
  • Maintain minimum clear space of 1× the star mark width on all sides
  • Use the standalone mark for small sizes (below 80px wide)
  • Scale the logo proportionally
  • Use high-resolution SVG/PNG files from the asset kit

Don't

  • Recolor the mark in non-brand colors
  • Stretch, rotate, or skew the wordmark
  • Place on low-contrast photographic backgrounds without an overlay
  • Separate "uzbek" from "trip" with line breaks in the wordmark
  • Add drop shadows, outlines, or effects to the logo

Chapter 06

Voice & Tone

uzbektrip.com speaks like a knowledgeable local friend — someone who has lived and traveled these routes and wants you to experience them fully.

Knowledgeable

We speak with authority on Uzbekistan. Every fact is verified, every recommendation is personal. We cite distances, opening times, and prices because travelers need specifics.

Warm

Uzbek hospitality — mehmon — is legendary. Our writing reflects this warmth. We write for curious people, not for algorithms. Sentences breathe; we don't rush.

Honest

We mention the bureaucratic friction at Tashkent airport. We warn about summer heat. We're not a tourism board. Travelers trust uzbektrip.com because we tell the full picture.

Multilingual Brand Name Rules

Always lowercase

  • Always written uzbektrip in lowercase — even at the start of a sentence
  • Domain always lower: uzbektrip.com
  • Exception: printed letterhead uses mixed case for legibility
  • Hashtags: #uzbektrip (all platforms)

Transliteration

  • Russian: uzbektrip.com — no translation needed
  • Uzbek: uzbektrip.com — same in Latin script
  • Arabic/Farsi contexts: use English romanization uzbektrip
  • Never translate the brand name; it is always uzbektrip
Chapter 07

Recommended Next Steps

A prioritized roadmap from brand book to live website.

1

Finalize & Export Logo Asset Kit

Export the brand logo in 5 variants (SVG + PNG 2×): primary on cream, reversed on lapis, reversed on dark, single mark, favicon.svg. Deliver in a shared design folder. This is the dependency for all other work.

2

Register Social Media Handles

Secure @uzbektrip on Instagram, Telegram, YouTube, Twitter/X, TikTok, and Facebook immediately — before launch. Even dormant handles prevent competitors from claiming the name. Set profile photos to the standalone star mark.

3

Migrate uzbekguide.html to uzbektrip.com Design System

The existing uzbekguide-homepage.html is an excellent prototype. Update the domain references, swap the wordmark to uzbektrip, confirm all CSS variables match this brand book, and re-test responsive behavior. The homepage skeleton is already production-quality.

4

Django + Wagtail Setup (Phase 1)

Follow the development blueprint in uzbekistan-tourism-website-guide.md. Priority: Dokploy Docker stack, wagtail-localize, wagtail-seo, and the SEO base.html template. Deliver Homepage + Samarkand + Bukhara + Tashkent city pages in EN within 8 weeks.

5

Commission Hero Photography

The brand lives or dies on imagery. Commission or license 20–30 hero photographs: Registan at golden hour, Khiva rooftops, Bukhara night market, Tashkent metro mosaic art. Always use real Uzbekistan photography — never stock. Unsplash provides a usable starting set; replace with original shots by Month 3.

6

Russian Translation (RU Locale)

Russian is the primary commercial language for CIS tourism to Uzbekistan. Add the RU locale via Wagtail admin after launch. Use DeepL for machine-translation drafts, then editorial review. RU locale should be live within 4 weeks of EN launch. All meta tags, OG data, and hreflang must be complete.

7

OG Image Template System

Build a reusable Open Graph image template (1200 × 630 px) in Figma or Canva: Lapis overlay on destination photo, uzbektrip logo bottom-left, city/article title in Cormorant Garamond. Generate one per major page. This directly improves click-through rates from Telegram and Instagram link shares.

8

Advertiser Rate Card

Design a one-page advertising rate card (PDF) using this brand system. The uzbekistan-tourism-website-guide.md defines 5 ad slots. Price slots competitively for Uzbek and regional travel advertisers. Distribute via email to hotels, tour operators, and SIM card providers (Ucell, Beeline).

9

Core Web Vitals Audit at Launch

Target LCP < 2.5 s, INP < 200 ms, CLS < 0.1 on mobile. The blueprint specifies Redis caching, WhiteNoise static compression, WebP images with explicit dimensions, and lazy loading. Run PageSpeed Insights on every city page before publishing.

10

Content Seed — 10 High-Intent Articles

Write the 7 highest-intent articles identified in the blueprint before Google indexing (visa-free list, e-visa guide, SIM card guide, Afrosiyob train, currency/UZS, best time to visit, Tashkent airport transfer). These rank within weeks and drive early organic traffic that seeds ad revenue.
